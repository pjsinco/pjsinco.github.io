Register for DO Day!

Plan your participation in the largest advocacy day for the osteopathic profession!

 

DO Day was transformed in 2021 to provide specialized learning tracks for physicians, students, and affiliate leaders. Check out the sessions that have been planned for DO Day 2022 specifically for affiliate leaders!
  • Deep Dive on the State Legislative Landscape
  • Enhancing Your Association’s Advocacy Program Through Collaboration
  • Deep Dive on the Federal Legislative Landscape
  • Scope of Practice Campaign Planning
  • Increasing Student and DO Engagement in State Advocacy
  • Leveraging Advocacy to Enhance Member Value

Remember, there are two ways to participate:

 

Virtual & In Person: Registration includes participation in both the virtual leadership and professional development conference scheduled on April 23-24, as well as in-person briefings and congressional meetings in Washington, DC, on Tuesday, April 26 and Wednesday, April 27. Please note: Space is limited for in-person congressional meetings. The deadline to register for this option is March 25, 2022.

 

Virtual Only: The leadership and professional development conference, set for April 23-24, will offer educational sessions focused on advocacy leadership, federal and state legislative priorities, regulatory and payment policy, state grassroots advocacy and more. Residents, students and affiliate leaders may register free of charge. On-demand content will be available for viewing on the conference platform through June 3.

 

PROMO CODES FOR EDs/CEOs: Check your email for a message from Nicole Blankenship, SVP, Affiliate Relations on Feb. 24 that included promo codes for State and Specialty College Affiliate Executive Directors/CEOs to receive discounted registration to participate in DO Day 2022. Review the program and reserve your spot today!

SOMA Audition How to Guide

The Student Osteopathic Medical Association (SOMA) created a great resource for incoming OMS IVs to prepare for audition rotations. Audition rotations are 2–4-week residencies where medical students “audition” for a hospital or healthcare system. Share this resource with medical students in your membership!

Nominations Now Being Accepted for the AOF/AAOA Donna Jones Moritsugu Memorial Award

The award honors the spouses of graduating Osteopathic medical students who best exemplify the type of immeasurable support and personal sacrifice that is crucial to their partner’s success. In partnership with the Advocates for the American Osteopathic Association, the American Osteopathic Foundation extends this prestigious award to spouses officially nominated by Osteopathic Colleges/Schools of Osteopathic Medicine across the country. More information and a link to the nomination portal can be found on the American Osteopathic Foundation’s website. Nominations will be accepted through Monday, May 16, 2022.

Kresge/Kellogg Community Innovations for Racial Equity - Through the Build Health Places Network, the Kresge Foundation and the W.K. Kellogg Foundation will provide funding, technical assistance, and a peer learning cohort to support race-conscious partnerships in minority communities. Ten awards will be made with up to $40,000 to support staff, $60,000 of in-kind technical support, and facilitated connections to a national network of peer support.

 

IHS Tribal Management Grant Program –The Indian Health Service (IHS) grant program anticipates making 15 awards of up to $150,000 each to tribes and tribal organizations developing a health management infrastructure. The program consists of four project types: feasibility study, planning, evaluation, and health management structure.

 

HRSA COVID-19 Coverage Assistance Fund - HRSA is providing claims reimbursement at the national Medicare rate for eligible health care providers administering vaccines to underinsured individuals. Click here to learn more.

 

Department of Health and Human Services – The Department of Health and Human Services We Can Do This Campaign has a new video about the importance of getting a COVID-19 booster. The national initiative is to increase confidence in COVID-19 vaccines and reinforce basic prevention measures.

 

Centers for Disease Control and Prevention - The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention’s Hear Her Campaign has launched a suite of materials for health care professionals. The Hear Her Campaign focuses on work across federal agencies to address maternal mortality and reduce health disparities.
